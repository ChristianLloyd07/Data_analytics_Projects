### Enrollment Performance Analysis Dashboard (Excel)  
HSL-Braille College Inc. | Data Analytics Portfolio Project

## Project Summary
This project demonstrates practical Data Analytics, Business Intelligence, and Operations Analysis skills through an end-to-end enrollment performance dashboard built entirely in Microsoft Excel. The dashboard analyzes historical enrollment data (2021–2025) to identify trends, performance gaps, capacity utilization, and retention risks, supporting data-driven decision-making for educational management.

## Business Problem
Educational institutions require accurate enrollment forecasting, retention monitoring, and capacity planning to remain sustainable. This project addresses the following business questions:

- How is enrollment trending year-over-year?
- What is the institution’s capacity utilization rate?
- Are enrollments driven by new students or returning students?
- Where are the drop-off points by grade level?
- Which senior high strands show the highest and lowest demand?
- Are retention and scholarship allocations aligned with institutional goals?

## Key Analytical Insights

# Enrollment Trend Analysis
- 2025 enrollment reached 744 students, reflecting a 4% YoY increase
- Overall growth from 2022–2025 remains flat, indicating stagnation
- A 6% decline in 2023 highlights enrollment volatility

## Capacity Utilization
- Current utilization rate: 65%
- Estimated maximum capacity: 1,150 students
- Identifies growth opportunities without infrastructure expansion

## New vs Returning Students
- New student intake declined to 22% in 2025
- Increasing reliance on returning students, posing long-term acquisition risks

## Student Retention Analysis
- 34% of students are one-time enrollees
- Indicates low retention rate and potential experience or engagement gaps

## Grade-Level Performance (2024 vs 2025)
- Significant enrollment declines:
- Grade 1: −15%
- Grade 11: −12%
- Grade 7: −3%
- Signals challenges in early entry and senior high transitions

## Scholarship Distribution Analysis
- 35% Regular students
- Remaining population distributed across ESC, QVR, and TLC
- Enables evaluation of financial aid impact on enrollment behavior

Senior High Strand Demand
- HUMSS: 43%
- STEM: 29%
- ABM: 16%
- ICT: 9%
- HES: 4%
- Supports program optimization and marketing alignment
